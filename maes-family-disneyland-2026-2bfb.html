Option 1  ·  Across the street  ·  1 room  ·  sleeps 6

Best Western Plus Park Place Inn & Mini Suites

The closest of the three to the parks, with the most flexible bedding

Best Western Park Place sits directly across Harbor Boulevard from Disneyland's main entrance — under a five-minute walk to the security check, the shortest of any option here. The Mini Suite layout is what makes it work for a family of five: two queen beds plus a separate queen-size sleeper sofa in a sitting area, sleeping up to six adults legally. Parents can take one queen, the older child the other, and the younger two share the sleeper.

The property is a workhorse, not a showpiece — clean rooms, friendly staff, a small heated pool, and a continental breakfast in the morning. What you're paying for is location and the legal occupancy for five.

Option 2  ·  Half-mile walk  ·  Studio suite  ·  Free breakfast

Home2 Suites by Hilton Anaheim Resort

All-suite layout, kitchenette, hot breakfast included for the family

Home2 Suites is Hilton's newer extended-stay brand, and the entire hotel is built as suites — every room has a separate sitting area, a kitchenette with full-size fridge and microwave, and a workspace. The Studio Suite for your group has two queen beds plus a full-size sleeper sofa, sleeping the family of five comfortably.

The free hot breakfast is the practical headline. For a family of five, breakfast at park gates or even quick-service spots runs $50–80 a day; eating before you leave the hotel saves real money over three mornings and gets you to the gate fed and ready. Just under half a mile to Disneyland's main entrance — about a 10-minute walk, or a quick ART shuttle ride.

Option 3  ·  Across the street  ·  Marriott Deluxe  ·  Built-in bunk bed

Courtyard by Marriott Anaheim Theme Park Entrance

Disney's only Deluxe-tier option of the three, with a kid-built room layout

The Courtyard sits across the street from Disneyland's main entrance, the same five-minute walk as Best Western. What sets it apart is Marriott's family-configured room: two queen beds plus a built-in bunk bed in the same room — purpose-designed for families with kids. Younger ones will see the bunks before they see anything else and want to climb in.

Disney rates the Courtyard at the Deluxe tier (their middle category between Moderate and Premium), which translates to newer finishes, a larger pool deck, an on-site Bistro for breakfast and dinner, and Marriott's tighter brand standards. The trade you're making for the extra $220 over Best Western is brand consistency and the bunk-bed feature, not location.

Park Hopper upgrade
+$900 for the family  ·  kids ages 3-9 free with the summer offer
With a Park Hopper, you can move between Disneyland Park and California Adventure on the same day after 11 AM — useful when one park has a ride the kids want and the other has shorter lines or character meets. Disney's Kids' Summer Ticket Offer covers the upgrade for kids 3-9 at no extra cost; you only pay for the three guests ages 10+ ($300 each = $900 total).
Lightning Lane Multi Pass
+$340 for the family · covers both park days
Skip the standby line at select headliner attractions and use the Lightning Lane entrance instead. Includes unlimited Disney PhotoPass downloads and AR PhotoPass Lenses across both park days. Honest trade — you can absolutely have a great trip without it by arriving at park opening (rope drop) and hitting headliners in the first 90 minutes. For peak summer crowds with a 4-year-old, the family that adds Lightning Lane will feel noticeably less in-line stress over the two days.
