Samaná sits on the northeastern peninsula of the Dominican Republic, a stretch of coast where the rainforest reaches the water and humpback whales pass through the bay between January and March. Cayo Levantado is a small island off the peninsula — one resort, one private beach, no town to leave for.

Two weeks here is a different kind of trip than the typical Punta Cana week. Slower. Quieter. Boat transfer from the mainland marks the shift; once you're on the island, that's where you stay.

Both packages below are priced for two adults sharing one room, fourteen nights, all-inclusive.

Cayo Levantado Resort — Junior Suite vs. Plunge Pool Suite
Option 1 · All-inclusive · 1 room · 2 adults
Cayo Levantado Resort — Junior Suite, Sea View
the room itself, with the bay through the window

The base sea-view suite gives you the location without paying for the pool deck. ~600 sq ft, king or two queens, a balcony or terrace facing Samaná Bay. You're a 90-second walk to the beach and the same distance to two of the six restaurants. Marble bath, soaking tub, the Bulgari amenity kit the property uses across all categories.

This is the right choice if you'll be spending most of your day out — at the beach, in the water, eating somewhere — and just need the room to be quiet, comfortable, and well-located when you come back.

Option 2 · All-inclusive · 1 room · 2 adults · plunge pool
Cayo Levantado Resort — Junior Suite, Sea View (Plunge Pool)
your own water, on your own terrace

Same suite footprint, same finishes — but with a private plunge pool on the terrace and a slightly better positioning along the property. The pool is the size of an oversized hot tub: enough to cool off in, deep enough to float, not a place to do laps. Most guests in this category use it in the late afternoon when the main beach gets busy or in the early morning before breakfast.

Honest trade: ~$1,400 more across the two weeks for the plunge pool. If you'll actually use it daily, it's worth it. If you'd rather spend that on excursions — a humpback whale tour off Samaná in early June isn't quite peak season, but it's still a possibility — start with Option 1.
