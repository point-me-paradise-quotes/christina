The Trip at a Glance

Seventeen Nights, Two Turns of the Caribbean

Drive to Galveston, board by 5 p.m., and don't touch your wallet again until you're home.

This is Regent Seven Seas' holiday sailing aboard Seven Seas Splendor — a ship of just 373 suites, every one with a private balcony, carrying 732 guests with 567 crew looking after them. You sail out of Galveston on Friday, December 11 and spend the next two and a half weeks tracing an arc through the Caribbean: Jamaica first, then east through Punta Cana, San Juan, and the small-harbor islands — Tortola, St. Maarten, St. Barts, Antigua — before turning for home with Christmas Day ashore in Grand Cayman. You're back at the Galveston pier at 7 a.m. on December 28.

The fare covers essentially everything: up to 85 shore excursions across the nine ports, every restaurant onboard including the specialty dining rooms, drinks from specialty coffee to fine wine and craft cocktails, gratuities, Wi-Fi, and valet laundry — which matters on a 17-night sailing. There is no drinks package to weigh, no excursion budget to manage, no envelope of tips to plan. The number on this page is the number.

Jamaica, Two Ways

December 14–15

The ship gives you Jamaica as an evening and a full day. Montego Bay runs 2 p.m. to 10 p.m. — late enough to have dinner ashore or watch the lights come up along the Hip Strip before walking back to the ship. Ocho Rios the next morning is the classic day: Dunn's River Falls, the Blue Hole, or the gardens at Konoko, all within a short drive of the pier, all covered by your included excursions.

Six Islands in Six Days

December 17–22

This is the stretch that separates a 17-night sailing from a week in the western Caribbean: Punta Cana, San Juan, Tortola, St. Maarten, St. Barts, and Antigua back to back. Old San Juan's blue cobblestones and El Morro are a ten-minute walk from the berth. Gustavia is the day to do very little, expensively and well — the ship anchors off a harbor lined with white-hulled yachts and red roofs. Antigua's early 7 a.m. arrival is your chance at English Harbour and Shirley Heights before the midday heat.

Christmas Morning in George Town

December 25

You wake on Christmas Day anchored off Grand Cayman — Seven Mile Beach, Stingray City sandbar, or simply a quiet morning ashore before dinner back on board that evening. The two sea days before it — Christmas Eve included — are when a 732-guest ship is at its best: the Constellation Theatre and lounges in full evening swing, the pool deck to yourself by mid-morning, and nowhere you have to be.
