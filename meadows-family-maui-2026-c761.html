Kā‘anapali — the west shore

Livelier and more walkable. About 45 minutes from the airport, with resorts lined along one long beach connected by a paved boardwalk — restaurants, shops, and Whalers Village all on foot.

Option 1 · Kā‘anapali · 1 room · 2 adults, 2 kids

Hyatt Regency Maui Resort & Spa

Penguins in the atrium, fantasy pools, and the lowest entry price here.

The Hyatt leans into a particular kind of family magic that has nothing to do with a kids' club. The atrium is home to resident penguins, flamingos, swans and koi, and the complimentary daily keiki programming — a wildlife tour to meet the birds, penguin feedings, lei making, hula and cultural classes — is the kind of thing a 6- and 8-year-old remember for years. The grounds are 40 oceanfront acres of meandering "fantasy" pools, a waterslide, and one of Maui's best-known luaus on site.

It's also the most affordable way onto Kā‘anapali Beach in this set, and it comes in two room layouts depending on how you want the four of you to sleep.

Option 2 · Kā‘anapali · 1 room · 2 adults, 2 kids

The Westin Maui Resort & Spa, Kā‘anapali

A working kids' club, two real beds, and an 87,000-square-foot pool.

The Westin solves the two things families weigh most. First, the Westin Family Kids Club is currently operating — a staffed drop-off program for ages 5 to 12 with counselor-led cultural activities and evening poolside movie screenings, so an afternoon to yourselves is genuinely on the table. Second, this room has two queen beds rather than a sofa bed, which over seven nights with a 6- and 8-year-old is the difference between sharing and not.

The pool complex runs to 87,000 square feet across multiple levels with a waterslide and a dedicated kids' pool. You're steps from Whalers Village for dinner and shopping, and directly on Kā‘anapali Beach with its calm swimming and the boardwalk running the length of the shore.

Wailea — the south shore

Sunny, sheltered, and spread out. About 35 minutes from the airport, with manicured resorts set back from quiet golden beaches — the calmer, more polished side of the island.

Option 3 · Wailea · 1 room · 2 adults, 2 kids

Wailea Beach Resort — Marriott

Built around a pool that an eight-year-old will not stop talking about.

If the trip is partly about the kids losing their minds at the pool, this is the property. The adventure pool complex has the longest waterslide on Maui (there's a 48-inch height requirement, so both your kids clear it), alongside smaller slides, a zero-entry splash section for easing in, and a lifeguard on duty. Two separate adults-only pools mean you can find quiet while the kids stay loud somewhere else.

It sits in Wailea near the Four Seasons, same sunny coast, with four restaurants and a Starbucks on site for the morning scramble. One thing to know plainly: there's no beach directly on the property — it's a short walk along the coastal path to Wailea and Ulua beaches.

Option 4 · Wailea · 1 room · 2 adults, 2 kids

Four Seasons Resort Maui at Wailea

The most inclusive week on the list — and the one with the least to think about.

This is the resort that earns its reputation by removing friction. The complimentary Kids For All Seasons club runs 9 to 5 at no charge — a genuine, currently-operating drop-off program for ages 5 to 12, which puts both your kids squarely in range. House cars shuttle you around Wailea, the lei greeting extends to the keiki, and the room amenities for children are unusually thoughtful.

Three pools sit above Wailea Beach, one of them adults-only with an infinity edge, plus a children's pool and a waterslide for the younger set. Ferraro's does oceanfront Italian; the beach itself is calm, raked, and walkable. It's the priciest option here, and what you're buying is the absence of nickel-and-diming on the things families use most.
