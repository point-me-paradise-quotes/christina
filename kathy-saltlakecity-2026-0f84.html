Your two weeks

A long stay, one base, and a city that puts mountains, canyons, and a walkable downtown all within reach.

Fourteen nights is enough time to stop being a visitor and start having a routine — a coffee place, a walk you do twice, a canyon drive on a clear morning. The plan below puts you in one downtown base for the whole stretch, so there's no mid-trip move, no second check-in, no living out of a half-unpacked bag.

The hotel below is my anchor recommendation for the two of you — priced for one room, two adults, for all fourteen nights. If you'd rather have a kitchen and more room to spread out for a stay this long, I've also pointed you toward vacation rentals further down, and I'm glad to pull specific options for you. Flights are a separate, optional add-on — your call whether to book them through me or on your own.

Where you'd stay

Two very different stays — one downtown and walkable, one out in Sandy near the canyons. Here's the honest trade between them.

TOP RECOMMENDATION Option 1 · 1 room · 2 adults · 14 nights

The Little America Hotel

Ten acres of private grounds in the middle of downtown.

Little America opened in 1952 as a single roadside stop and grew, over seventy years, into a 17-story downtown landmark run by the same Utah family that owns the Grand America across the street. It has held a AAA Four Diamond rating for more than two decades, and the interiors lean into it — French Richelieu furnishings, silk wall coverings, English wool carpet, a fireplace going in the lobby.

For a June stay, the property's own pitch is the Garden rooms, and it holds up: the hotel sits on ten acres of landscaped grounds, which no other downtown SLC hotel can claim, so you get a pocket of quiet and greenery without leaving the center of the city. The half-indoor, half-outdoor pool looks out over the skyline and runs year-round; the seasonal outdoor pool opens for the summer with poolside food and drink service.

Location does a lot of work here for two weeks without a rush. You're about a mile from Temple Square, steps from the City Creek Center shops and the Salt Palace, and right by the TRAX light-rail Courthouse Station — so a car is optional, not required. The airport is roughly ten minutes out, and the Cottonwood canyons and their ski resorts are about thirty minutes by car if you want a mountain day.

Option 2 · 1 room · 2 adults · 14 nights

Best Western Plus CottonTree Inn

The value play — out in Sandy, closer to the canyons.

This is the practical, lower-cost alternative, and it earns its place on a different set of strengths than Little America. It sits in Sandy, about twenty miles and twenty-five minutes south of downtown along I-15 — suburban rather than central, but recently renovated throughout, with new beds, new furniture, 40-inch TVs, and a mini-fridge in every room. Guest scores run high (8.8), and couples rate it well.

Two things come free here that you'd pay for at a downtown hotel: self-parking and a full hot breakfast every morning, served 6 to 10. Over fourteen nights, that's real money back in your pocket — roughly $250 in parking alone that you won't spend. There's an indoor pool and hot tub, a fitness center, and laundry on site.

The honest trade: you're not in the city. Downtown SLC, Temple Square, and the City Creek shops are a 25-minute drive — though a TRAX light-rail station sits about two miles away and runs straight into the center, so a car isn't strictly required. What Sandy gives you instead is position: you're noticeably closer to the Cottonwood canyons and the ski resorts (Snowbird, Brighton, Solitude, Alta), near a ski-bus stop, and within a few minutes of the Living Planet Aquarium, South Towne Center, and Rio Tinto Stadium. If your two weeks lean toward mountains and day trips over downtown evenings, this base actually fits better — and costs less.
