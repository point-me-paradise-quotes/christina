The Flow

How the trip unfolds

The shape of your fifteen days, leg by leg. Each section is built to give you depth in one place before the next move.

1

The Amalfi Coast

Praiano · September 20 — 25 · five nights

You leave Houston Saturday evening on ITA Airways, change planes once in Rome, and land in Naples Sunday afternoon. A private driver meets you at NAP and runs you down the SS-163 to Praiano — a working village west of Positano, perched between Positano's drama and Amalfi's history. Quieter than its famous neighbors, with the same sea.

You'll stay in a one-bedroom apartment with a private terrace, a three-minute walk from the village center and eight minutes from the beach. Five days to settle into the rhythm: cliffside mornings, long lunches, sunset walks down to Marina di Praia. Day trips to Capri, Ravello, and Amalfi Town from this base.

2

The Cycladic Heartland

Naxos · September 25 — 30 · five nights

Friday morning a private driver takes you back to Naples. You fly Aegean to Athens, connect to Olympic Air, and land in Naxos by mid-afternoon. A short ride to Plaka Beach — a four-star beachfront property, white sand at the doorstep, daily breakfast included.

Naxos is the largest of the Cyclades and the least polished of the famous islands. Mountain villages inland — Apiranthos, Halki, Filoti — long sand beaches along the west coast, and Mt. Zas where the ancients said Zeus was born. The pace is slower than Santorini and the prices are gentler. We strongly recommend a rental car for the inland and beach-hopping days.

3

The Caldera

Santorini · September 30 — October 4 · four nights

Wednesday afternoon a fast ferry runs you across to Santorini in just over an hour. A private driver takes you up to Imerovigli — the highest point on the caldera rim, often called the Balcony of Santorini, and the quieter alternative to Oia and Fira.

You'll stay in an adults-only cave hotel carved into the volcanic cliff. Loft suite with king bed, sea views, breakfast served on your private terrace. Four full days for the iconic moments — caldera sunsets, Oia at dawn, a volcano boat day, the wineries — without overstaying a small island.

4

One Night in Rome

Fiumicino · October 4 — 5 · one night

Saturday morning, a non-stop flight from Santorini lands you at Rome Fiumicino just past noon. Your hotel is connected directly to Terminal 3 by a covered walkway — five minutes from gate to room. If you'd like to spend the afternoon in Rome city center, the Leonardo Express train runs from the same terminal and reaches central Rome in 32 minutes.

Sunday morning, a five-minute walk back to the terminal. ITA non-stop to Houston, landing late afternoon.
