Where you'll stay

Five all-inclusives in Cabo San Lucas, sorted from most affordable to most premium — here's how they differ, with honest trade-offs for each.

All five are all-inclusive and all in Cabo San Lucas, but they're not interchangeable. The real decision comes down to three things: all-ages vs. adults-only, how lively you want the resort itself, and how close you want to be to the marina, where the Bisbee weigh-ins, registration, and ceremonies all happen at Puerto Paraíso. Marina Fiesta sits right on the marina; the others are a 5-minute cab or a cheap public bus from it. Here's the honest read on each:

Riu Santa Fe
The value play · all ages

For: the lowest price by a wide margin, a big lively resort with a water park and nightclub, and the room categories Sean already knows. The Riu Party room ties into the on-site pool-party scene.

Against: it's large and busy rather than intimate, family-and-kids energy is part of the mix, and dinner restaurants require formal attire.

Riu Palace Cabo San Lucas
The step-up · all ages

For: the same beach and neighborhood as the Santa Fe with a noticeably more polished, all-suite product — junior suites with sitting areas, up to swim-up and jacuzzi categories. The natural upgrade without a budget leap.

Against: still a sizable resort, not a boutique, and several ocean-view categories are "subject to availability" until confirmed.

Riu Palace Baja California
All-suite · adults only

For: 18-and-over throughout, every room a junior suite or larger, with free access to the Santa Fe's water park, parties, and Pacha nightclub when you want the energy — and a calmer home base when you don't.

Against: the two Elite Club tiers jump sharply in price, and it's the same short-ride-not-a-walk distance to the marina.

Marina Fiesta
On the marina · all ages

For: the only one sitting right on the Cabo San Lucas marina, steps from Puerto Paraíso and the weigh-ins — walk out the door into the tournament action. All-ages, suite-style rooms, family-friendly with kids staying free.

Against: marina-facing, not beachfront ("off beach"), so there's no sand out front — you'd head to Médano for beach time.

Breathless Cabo San Lucas
Newest & most social · adults only

For: opposite El Médano and a short walk to the marina, the newest and most modern-feeling of the group — the most social, party-leaning resort, with a full spa and nightly shows.

Against: the priciest of the beach resorts, and the high-energy vibe is a poor fit if anyone wants quiet.

A note on Bisbee week

The tournament lives at the marina — registration, the captains meeting, opening ceremonies, and the 2:00–9:00 PM weigh-ins all happen at or in front of Puerto Paraíso, and the 2026 Black & Blue runs October 19–24, right inside your week. Marina Fiesta is the only one of the five sitting directly on the marina; the rest are a 5-minute cab or a cheap public bus from it. Weigh how much the resort's own setting matters — beach out front, adults-only calm, on-site energy — against how often you'll be heading to the docks. All five make the marina easy.

Option 1 · All ages · 1 room · 2 adults

Riu Santa Fe — All Inclusive

A big, busy beach resort a short walk from the marina action.

Riu Santa Fe sits about a 12-minute walk from El Médano Beach, on the strip of Riu resorts just outside the heart of Cabo San Lucas. The marina and Puerto Paraíso — where the Bisbee weigh-ins, registration, and ceremonies all take place — are roughly 1.5 miles away: a 5-minute cab, a $1–2 public bus from just outside the Riu gate every 15–30 minutes, or a 30–40 minute beach walk (doable but slow on coarse, sloped sand). This is the high-energy end of the all-inclusive spectrum: a five-star, all-ages property with a water park and slide, an infinity pool, the Pacha nightclub running six days a week, and 18-plus RIU Pool Parties when you want them.

Food runs across eight restaurants — La Baja California, Kulinarium, Sakura, Carusso, La Misión, Torote, Pepe's Food, and the Tiki Taco snack spots — plus buffet breakfast, lunch, and dinner, with themed dinners twice a week. The all-inclusive covers daytime entertainment, live shows, a kids' club for ages 4–12, land sports like tennis and volleyball, and steam-room access.

Honest trade: this is a large, lively resort — efficient rather than intimate, and at its liveliest around the pool and nightclub. Dinner restaurants require formal attire, and spa treatments, golf, snorkel, massage, and horseback riding carry extra charges. The fitness center and steam room are adults-only.

Riu Palace Cabo San Lucas — All Inclusive

The same stretch of beach as the Santa Fe, stepped up a tier.

Riu's upscale Palace tier on the same Cabo San Lucas beachfront — the natural step up from the Santa Fe if you want a more polished room and a calmer feel without leaving the neighborhood or the marina access. Guests also get free access to the other Riu hotels in the destination (restrictions apply, details at check-in).

The rooms here lead with junior suites — most with a sitting area, balcony or terrace, and a step up in finish from the Santa Fe's standard doubles. The category ladder runs from the base Junior Suite through ocean-view suites to a swim-up suite and a jacuzzi suite, so there's room to trade up if the view or the layout matters to you.

Honest trade: this is the most affordable way into Riu's Palace product on this beach, but it's still a sizable resort rather than a boutique — and several room categories list view as "subject to availability," so a specific ocean view isn't locked until confirmed.

Option 3 · Adults only (18+) · 1 room · 2 adults

Riu Palace Baja California — Adults Only

An adults-only Palace for couples who want the resort calmer.

The adults-only Palace in Cabo San Lucas — a five-star beachfront resort, 18-and-over throughout, with free access to sister Riu hotels in the destination. That access is worth noting: it includes the water park, adults-only theme parties, and Pacha nightclub over at the Riu Santa Fe, plus tennis courts at the Riu Palace Cabo San Lucas. Playa El Médano is about 2.2 km away; the marina and Puerto Paraíso (the Bisbee hub) are a short cab or bus ride, same as the other Rius. A good fit if the group would rather skip the family-and-water-park energy on-site and keep their own resort grown-up.

Five restaurants (Promenade, Yu Hi, Krystal, Guacamole, and Sofia), nightly shows, a nightclub, a pool bar, and a spa with steam room and Jacuzzi. The all-inclusive covers all meals and drinks, a daily-restocked minibar and liquor dispenser, snorkeling, nightly entertainment, and a daily activities program. Every category is a junior suite or larger, each with a sitting area, bathrobes, and a private bath with shower and tub.

Honest trade: all-suite and adults-only is the draw, but several views list as "subject to availability," and the jump to the two Elite Club tiers is a real step up in price. Like the other Rius, it's a short ride rather than a walk to the marina and the tournament action.

Option 4 · All ages · 1 room · 2 adults

Marina Fiesta Resort & Spa — All Inclusive

On the marina itself — steps from Puerto Paraíso and the boats.

Marina Fiesta sits right on the Cabo San Lucas marina, at the edge of the Puerto Paraíso shopping and entertainment plaza — the spot where the Bisbee registration, ceremonies, and weigh-ins all take place. Of the options here, it's the one you can roll out of and be in the middle of the tournament action on foot. It's an all-ages, family-friendly all-inclusive built around suite-style rooms.

The room categories run from the Nautical Junior Suite up through one- and two-bedroom suites, so there's room to spread out for a couple or stretch to fit more. The all-inclusive covers meals and drinks; specific dining and amenity details are confirmed at booking.

Honest trade: this is a marina-facing resort, not a beach resort — the listing notes it as "off beach," so you're trading sand-out-front for being right on the water and the marina nightlife. For a group whose week revolves around the boats and the weigh-ins, that's arguably the point; if you want to wake up on Médano, one of the beach resorts fits better.

Option 5 · Adults only (18+) · 1 room · 2 adults

Breathless Cabo San Lucas Resort & Spa

Adults-only and social, opposite El Médano and next to the marina.

The most upscale of the four and the most adults-only in feel — Breathless sits opposite El Médano beach, next to the Los Cabos marina, within walking distance of the city center, the nightlife strip, and the dining and shopping areas. It's the AMR Collection's social, energetic brand, so expect nightly shows, a nightclub, theme events, and a lively pool scene rather than a quiet retreat.

The all-inclusive is broad: all meals and snacks, unlimited national and international drinks, 24-hour room service, and waiter service at the pool and beach. Beyond the food there's a full spa (sauna, steam, hydromassage, body and facial treatments), a fitness center with a personal trainer, yoga, cooking classes, a bar route, and six restaurants — Bites, Fish Nets, Kibbeh, Spumante, Spoon, and the Barefoot Grill. Two room categories are offered: the base Allure marina-view room and the upgraded Xhale Club concierge level.

Honest trade: this is the priciest option and the social, party-leaning one — a plus for two couples who want energy, less so if anyone's after calm. It's a touch farther from the airport (about 50 km) than the Rius, and fishing, snorkel, and some services carry extra charges.
