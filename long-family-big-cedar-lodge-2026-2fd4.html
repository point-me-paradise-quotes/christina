Your Trip

December on the Ozarks lake — the right call

Big Cedar Lodge sits on 4,600 acres above Table Rock Lake in the Missouri Ozarks. Summer crowds are long gone by December, holiday lighting runs property-wide, and the lake sits quiet in a way peak season never allows. Bass Pro's collection of restaurants — Top of the Rock among them — means real dining options beyond the lodge's own kitchen, and the property's trails, pools, and beach give six people plenty of room to move.

Three cabin options are outlined below, ranging from a cozy Camp Long Creek family base to a lakeside cottage with an infinity pool a short walk away. All three sleep your group; the differences are location, layout, bedroom configuration, and finish level. Review each, walk the virtual tours, and let Christina know which direction works for you.

Your Options

Three cabins, all available Dec 3–6

Camp Long Creek Two-Bedroom Camp Cabin

Located within Camp Long Creek — Big Cedar's most family-centered village

Camp Long Creek was designed with families in mind from the start — two king bedrooms on either end of the cabin, a queen sleeper sofa in the living area, and a screened-in porch that works well even in early December. A full kitchen means you're not locked into restaurant meals every morning. The private beach, kiddie pool, and lazy river are steps away, and the cabin is dog-friendly.

Two-Bedroom Cabin

Elevated finish, private deck — the step-up option for the group

This cabin is the choice when you want a higher finish level and a bit more separation from the activity zones. Two king bedrooms anchor the layout. Bass Pro Shops Lakeside Two-Bedroom Family Cottage

Steps from the water's edge and an infinity pool — the most lake-focused option

This cottage sits in close walking distance to both the water and an infinity pool — the most lake-adjacent of the three options. Inside, the bedroom configuration is different from the others: one king room with a private bath and a second bedroom with two queen beds and its own bath, which makes it a better fit for the kids than a sleeper sofa. The wood-burning fireplace in the living area earns its keep in December. The covered porch and gas grill round out a setup that works for three nights without feeling cramped.
