Your Celebration Trip

Two destinations. One milestone.

Both packages are priced for 4 adults — 2 parents, 2 graduates — 5 nights all-inclusive.

A graduation is worth doing properly. I put real research into this quote before presenting two options — each with a distinct personality, in two different countries, at two meaningfully different price points. One is a newly opened design-forward resort on a stretch of coast most travelers haven't found yet. The other is a larger, high-energy all-suite property built for exactly this kind of family celebration.

I looked at several destinations before landing here. Aruba priced out significantly higher for a group of four in July with no comparable upgrade in experience. Isla Mujeres — while the water at Playa Norte is genuinely among the most beautiful in Mexico — doesn't have the all-inclusive resort infrastructure this trip calls for. A number of other Caribbean destinations were ruled out because reaching them from Houston requires two connections, which adds meaningful risk and travel time to a five-night trip. Costa Rica was on the list early, but July sits squarely in its rainy season — the water clarity and beach conditions aren't what you'd want for a celebration built around the beach. Both options below solve for all of that.

The July 9–14 dates are an example — I chose them because the air pricing looked strong and they made the math clean. If you want to look at different dates, just mention it in the notes below or reach out directly and I'll come back with options quickly.

A note on sargassum

Sargassum — brown Atlantic seaweed — washes ashore across the Caribbean and Mexico between April and October, with July squarely in peak season. It has become a genuine issue along much of the Riviera Maya, the Yucatan Hotel Zone, and parts of the eastern Caribbean. I research it actively for every beach recommendation I make.

Both properties below were selected in part because their geography provides natural protection from the heaviest drift patterns. I can't promise a completely clear beach on any given day — no one can. What I can tell you is that both locations consistently outperform most of the region, and I'll be monitoring conditions before your trip. If anything changes materially, I'll contact you directly.

Option 1 — Dominican Republic
Option 1 · 4 adults · 5 nights all-inclusive

Zemi Miches Punta Cana All-Inclusive Resort

Curio Collection by Hilton · Playa Esmeralda, Miches, Dominican Republic

Opened in 2025, Zemi Miches is Hilton's first Curio Collection all-inclusive in the Dominican Republic — and it arrived fully formed. Set on 2,000 feet of Playa Esmeralda inside a protected bay, this is a property built around the idea that luxury and cultural authenticity don't compete. Every detail — the architecture, the art, the food, the daily programming — draws from the Taino heritage of the DR, creating a sense of place that most all-inclusives never attempt.

Dramatic multilevel pools, fire-pit lounges floating in the water, rattan cabanas framing a calm turquoise bay, mountain views behind. The overall feeling is intimate and unhurried — a 500-room property that manages to feel like a private retreat. Fourteen globally inspired restaurants and bars span Thai street food, live-fire Dominican cooking, fresh-pressed juice markets, and a rooftop pool bar. The Acana Spa draws from ancestral Taino healing rituals. Evenings bring live performances at an outdoor theater. Paddleboarding, kayaking, and snorkeling fill the days.

On sargassum: Miches sits inside a protected bay on the northeastern DR coast. The peninsula's geography diverts Atlantic drift northward before it reaches Playa Esmeralda — one of the most consistently clear stretches in the entire country, even during the 2025 record-high sargassum season.

One of our advisors returned from Zemi Miches this month — on her own time, her own choice. She has not stopped talking about it. When a travel professional visits a resort without being asked and comes back genuinely enthusiastic, that tells you something the brochure can't.

Option 2 — Mexico
Option 2 · 4 adults · 5 nights all-inclusive · transfers included

Catalonia Grand Costa Mujeres All Suites & Spa

All-Inclusive · Costa Mujeres, Mexico · Transfers Included

The Catalonia Grand Costa Mujeres is a 496-suite all-inclusive set directly on a private white-sand beach north of Cancun — one of the most naturally protected stretches of coastline on the Yucatan Peninsula. It manages to feel both expansive and energetic: large enough to have everything your group needs across five nights, with enough variety that no two days feel the same.

At the center of the property is La Quinta Avenida — a pedestrian street modeled on Playa del Carmen's famous Fifth Avenue — lined with specialty restaurants, bars, and boutiques. It gives the resort an energy and variety that most all-inclusives simply can't replicate. Days flow naturally: beach in the morning, a specialty restaurant for lunch, the pools in the afternoon, the spa before dinner, live entertainment in the evening. Fourteen dining venues span Asian teppanyaki, Italian, Mexican, seafood, steakhouse, a gourmet buffet, a food truck, and a coffee shop. Two large pools with swim-up bars and a nightclub give the graduates what they're looking for. The parents have the Alegria Spa and genuinely great food.

On sargassum: Costa Mujeres faces slightly northwest rather than directly into the Caribbean, which gives it natural protection from prevailing Atlantic currents. Even on heavier days, dedicated beach cleanup crews have swimming areas clear by 9 AM — it consistently outperforms Cancun's Hotel Zone and the Riviera Maya.
