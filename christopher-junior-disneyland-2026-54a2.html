The plan

Two nights in the parks, three nights of sleep close by — pick the hotel and you're set.

This quote is built for the two of you — one adult and one six-year-old — sharing one room for three nights, Friday June 19 through Monday June 22. The park days are the same in either option: a 2-Day Park Hopper that lets you bounce between Disneyland and California Adventure across Saturday and Sunday.

What changes between the two options below is only the hotel — where you sleep, what you pay, and how close you are to the gates. Both rooms sleep four, so there's room to spread out even with just the two of you. Pick the one that fits, add Lightning Lane if you want to skip the standby lines, and send it over.

Hilton Anaheim

The big convention-district tower with the resort-style pool deck.
777 W Convention Way, Anaheim, CA
1 / 15

The Hilton Anaheim is the full-service high-rise option — a large hotel with a heated outdoor pool, an on-site restaurant, a fitness center, and the kind of lobby that feels like a small airport in the good sense: easy to find your way, plenty of room. It sits in the convention-center pocket just south of the parks, a short ride or a roughly 15-minute walk to the Disneyland gates.

For a dad traveling with a young kid, the appeal is space and amenities under one roof — somewhere to swim and burn off energy at the end of a park day, room service when nobody wants to leave again, and a real restaurant downstairs.

Option 2 · 1 room · sleeps 4

Best Western Plus Park Place Inn

The walk-across-the-street option, right at the Harbor Boulevard gate.
1544 S Harbor Blvd, Anaheim, CA
1 / 12

The Best Western Plus Park Place Inn trades the big-tower amenities for something a lot of Disneyland families value more: location. It sits on Harbor Boulevard directly across from the Disneyland entrance — close enough that you can walk back midday for a nap or a swim and return to the parks without it being a whole production.

It's a smaller, simpler property — a Mini Suite layout with a refrigerator and microwave in the room, included breakfast, and a pool. With a six-year-old, the ability to walk back to the room in a few minutes when the day catches up to them is worth a lot.

The honest trade: The Hilton is bigger, cheaper at the room-only rate, and has the better pool and on-site dining — but you're a 15-minute walk or a short drive from the gates. The Best Western costs more but puts you directly across the street from the entrance, with breakfast and an in-room fridge built in. With a six-year-old, that walk-back-anytime proximity is the thing to weigh against the price difference.
