The sailing

Drive to the port. No flights, no transfers, no airport mornings.

This is a seven-night round-trip out of Galveston aboard MSC Seascape — the Seaside EVO-class ship MSC moved to the Texas coast, christened by Sophia Loren in 2022 and carrying around 4,540 guests. The rhythm of the week is built around three sea days and three ports: two days at sea to settle in, then Costa Maya, Roatán, and Cozumel back-to-back midweek, then a final sea day to wind down before Sunday morning in Galveston.

Both options below are for the same cabin — a Deluxe Balcony on the Aurea Experience tier (decks 9–15), priced for two guests sharing. The difference between them is what's bundled on top: the second adds a drinks and WiFi promotion. Pick the one that fits how you like to sail.

The three ports

What each stop is actually known for — so you know which excursions are worth the early alarm.

Costa Maya is the longest day ashore (ten hours) and the most laid-back. The beach at Mahahual runs shallow for a long stretch — you can walk out on a sandbar — which makes it easy for swimming but flat for snorkeling unless you head to the reef. Mayan sites at Chacchoben are about an hour inland for those who want ruins over beach bars.

Roatán sits on the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef, the second-largest reef system in the world, and it's the diving and snorkeling highlight of the week. The town immediately around the port is unremarkable; the payoff is a short cab ride out to West Bay Beach or a reef tour. This is the day to book something rather than wander.

Cozumel is the polished, easy port — strong snorkeling at Chankanaab, a quick ferry to the mainland for Tulum or cenotes if you want a bigger day, and a walkable town for those who'd rather shop and eat than tour.
