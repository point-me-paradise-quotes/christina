Sandals Royal Bahamian

A historic Cable Beach hideaway with its own offshore island

Of everything I'm sending, this is the option I'd push hardest if you want a true couples' week. Sandals Royal Bahamian sits on the long crescent of Cable Beach in Nassau — a property with real history (the original Balmoral Club hosted the Duke and Duchess of Windsor) but renovated to feel current rather than dated. The whole resort is for couples only. No kids, no spring break, no piped-in pool music when you're trying to read.

What sets it apart from every other Sandals: Barefoot Cay, their private offshore island. A short water taxi from the resort lands you on a separate cay with a Zen garden, two beaches (one for swimming, one for snorkeling), a spa pavilion, and food trucks for casual lunches. It's the closest thing to a stay-on-resort excursion in the Caribbean.

Top-shelf liquor, Robert Mondavi wines, in-room minibar, ten restaurants, and round-trip airport transfers are all genuinely included — this is one of the few all-inclusives where there's no upcharge dance.

Zemi Miches Punta Cana

A new resort on a quieter Dominican coastline

Zemi Miches opened in 2025 as Hilton's first Curio Collection all-inclusive in the Dominican Republic. It sits on Playa Esmeralda — a stretch of unspoiled coastline north of the better-known Punta Cana resort strip, near Redonda Mountain. The whole property leans into Dominican and Taíno cultural identity (the name Zemi is the Taíno word for "sacred place") with a signature restaurant called Anacaona that serves locally inspired cooking, and a spa built around Taíno-inspired treatments.

The honest trade vs. Sandals: Zemi is family-friendly rather than couples-only, and the airport transfer is meaningfully longer — about 90 minutes from PUJ vs. 25 minutes to the standard Bávaro strip — because Miches is genuinely off the beaten path. The reward is a quieter beach, fewer crowds, and a property that feels new and design-forward rather than pre-renovation Caribbean. Some guests note the beach directly in front is rocky in spots; a 10-minute walk reveals the wider sandy stretch.

Eleven dining venues, four pools (including a rooftop infinity pool for adults), a waterpark for kids, and beachfront bungalows with private plunge pools for the upgrade-curious.

Cruise from Galveston

Drive to the port. No flights, no transfers, no airport mornings.

Two ships are doing the Galveston run this summer. Both are good in different ways. Mariner of the Seas is a refurbished modern classic — smaller (3,114 guests at double occupancy), 2003-built, given a $120 million Royal Amplification in 2018 and another light refresh in March 2026. Symphony of the Seas is the bigger, newer Oasis-class option (over 5,500 guests, 2018-built) with more onboard pretty much everything: more pools, more dining, more entertainment, longer itineraries. Mariner is "I want a cruise without committing a week." Symphony is "I want the whole show."

Other Directions Worth a Look

If none of the above is calling, here's where I'd point next.

Mexico

Cancún & Playa Mujeres, ~2½ hr nonstop

The Mexican Caribbean has had a strong run of new properties built specifically for couples. A few I'd shortlist:

  • Atelier Playa Mujeres — Mexican-owned design-forward all-inclusive
  • Hotel Xcaret México — cultural concept; included access to Xcaret theme parks
  • SLS Playa Mujeres — newer luxury all-inclusive on the quieter peninsula
  • Excellence Playa Mujeres — adults-only, classic luxury all-inclusive

Jamaica

Montego Bay or Negril, ~3½ hr nonstop

United runs IAH↔Montego Bay year-round. Jamaica's all-inclusive density is the highest in the Caribbean — Sandals has multiple properties on the island including the flagship Sandals Royal Caribbean and Sandals South Coast (a peaceful estate with overwater bungalows).

  • Best for those who want a Sandals experience but a different vibe than Nassau
  • Stronger green/mountain backdrop than the Bahamas

Grand Cayman

Seven Mile Beach, ~3 hr nonstop

Different model: Grand Cayman doesn't really do all-inclusives, but the resort experience is exceptional. Think Kimpton Seafire, Ritz-Carlton Grand Cayman, Palm Heights — sophisticated, design-driven, with a calm and safe island culture. Food scene punches above its weight.

  • Better for couples who'd rather choose restaurants than have meals included
  • Seven Mile Beach is genuinely one of the best beaches in the Caribbean
