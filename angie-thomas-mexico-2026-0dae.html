Two islands of the same coast

Both adults-only, both on the Caribbean — but they ask for different kinds of weeks.

These two are genuinely different trips, and the choice comes down to the kind of week you want rather than a better-or-worse call. Excellence Playa Mujeres is the big, polished, do-everything resort — a private two-mile beach, a roster of restaurants and pools, and the full all-inclusive machine running around you. Privilege Aluxes is the opposite instinct: a smaller adults-only hotel on a quiet island, steps from one of the region's best beaches and a short walk from a real town.

I've laid out the honest trade in each card below — meals, transfers, payment timing, and how you actually get there. Read both, then choose the one that's calling and the rate that fits; the running total updates as you go.

The biggest difference: how you get there

Excellence Playa Mujeres is on the mainland, about a 30–45 minute drive north of Cancún airport — and round-trip transfers are included. No ferry, no extra legs. Privilege Aluxes is on Isla Mujeres, an island reached by a short ferry from the mainland (roughly 20 minutes by car to the port, then a 15–20 minute ferry, then a brief taxi to the hotel). It's an easy, scenic trip that thousands make daily — but it is more steps than a straight drive, and transfers/ferry for Aluxes aren't included in the rate. Ask me and I'll arrange the full door-to-door for either.

Option 2 · Adults-only · Island · Reached by ferry

Privilege Aluxes

Isla Mujeres · Playa Norte · short ferry from Cancún · walk to town

Grand Platinum Suite with a Private Rooftop Pool

The intimate island alternative. Privilege Aluxes is a smaller adults-only hotel on the northern tip of Isla Mujeres, right at Playa Norte — consistently rated one of the best beaches in the Cancún area, with calm, shallow turquoise water. It's a short, scenic ferry from the mainland, and a few minutes' walk from downtown Hidalgo Street, its restaurants and bars. Where Excellence is a self-contained resort, Aluxes puts you on a real island with a town at your doorstep.

Your suite is the Grand Platinum Suite with a Private Rooftop Pool: a two-story suite with a private rooftop pool, a terrace with Caribbean views, a separate bedroom with a King bed, and a living room. On-site dining runs through Sabor (buffet and international), the Asian-inspired Satay, and the Playa Norte Beach Club on the sand. The honest trade: it's smaller and quieter with fewer on-site options than Excellence, and getting there involves the ferry — but that's exactly what gives it the island feel.

Best for: a quieter, more local week — one of the area's best beaches, a walkable town, and an intimate hotel over a big resort. Worth it if you don't mind the ferry.
