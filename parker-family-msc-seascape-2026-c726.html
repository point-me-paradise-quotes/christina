MSC Seascape

Seaside EVO class — one of MSC’s newest flagships, purpose-built for Caribbean sailing.

MSC Seascape was christened in New York City on December 7, 2022, then repositioned to Galveston for Caribbean sailings — meaning no flights, no airport mornings, just a drive to the port. At 170,000 gross tons and 1,112 feet, she is the longest ship in the MSC fleet. She holds 4,540 passengers at double occupancy and carries a crew of 1,648. The ship is designed around New York City as a theme: there is a Statue of Liberty replica at the center of the casino, Broadway-style shows in the theater, and the Robotron thrill ride on the upper decks. Specialty dining includes a teppanyaki restaurant (Kaito), a steakhouse (Butcher’s Cut), a seafood restaurant (Ocean Cay), a sushi bar, and a Latin street food concept. Big enough to keep the whole group busy; well-organized enough to find quiet corners when you want them.

Understanding Your Experience Tier

Three levels, meaningfully different — here is what each one actually includes.

Premium

Aurea Experience

  • Best balcony cabin locations on the ship — highest-demand areas reserved for Aurea
  • Priority boarding and luggage drop-off at embarkation
  • Anytime dining (MyChoice) — no fixed seating, dine when you want
  • Access to the Thermal Spa area (adult-only, once per day)
  • Access to the exclusive Aurea solarium (adults only)
  • Welcome Prosecco and chocolates on arrival
  • Bathrobes and slippers in cabin
  • Complimentary continental breakfast room service delivery
  • 24-hour room service with free delivery (food items charged individually)
  • Spa discounts, 20% off pre-paid specialty dining packages
  • One free cruise change (30 days before sailing)
Drinks are not included with Aurea — that benefit was discontinued in 2022. A drinks package can be added at a discount when booking.
Mid-tier — most popular for families

Fantastica Experience

  • Choose your specific cabin and location within the category (more options than Bella, but not the best positions — those go to Aurea)
  • Dining time priority — Fantastica guests get first pick of early or late fixed seating over Bella guests
  • Complimentary continental breakfast room service delivery
  • 24-hour room service with free delivery (food items charged individually)
  • 20% off pre-paid specialty dining packages
  • One free cruise change (30 days before sailing)
Entry-level — read carefully before selecting

Bella Experience

  • MSC assigns your specific stateroom up to 4 days before departure — no control over deck or location
  • Possible placements: forward, aft, or near high-traffic venues (nightclub above, busy corridors)
  • Dining time: you can submit a preference for early or late seating, but Fantastica and Aurea guests are served first
  • Continental breakfast room service available; delivery fee applies
  • Cruise changes for a fee
  • Lowest rate in each balcony category
Two things the Parker family should know about Bella balconies First, Bella balcony cabins sometimes have a metal railing instead of glass — younger children (roughly 6 and under) cannot see over it when seated, and it can make the balcony feel less useful. Second, for groups of 3–4 in one cabin, MSC may split you into two cabins on the same deck and side if availability requires it. Neither happens every time, but both are worth knowing before you commit to Bella.
