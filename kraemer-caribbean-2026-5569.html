Destination 1

Grenada

The "spice island" — quieter and less developed than its Caribbean neighbors. Known for nutmeg plantations, untouched beaches on the north coast, and a slow, unhurried rhythm. Best fit for travelers who want fewer crowds and natural beauty over big-resort polish.

Option 1 · Boutique · 12 rooms · north coast

Petite Anse Hotel

an upscale boutique near Sauteurs

Petite Anse is a small, owner-run hotel on Grenada's far north coast, near Duquesne and Levera Beaches. Twelve rooms, a poolside bar, a terrace overlooking the Caribbean, and a kitchen that does its own thing rather than chasing the buffet-resort formula. 9.8 across 132 reviews — the highest-rated property on the island that isn't pricing on last-minute distress.

This is a different category of property than the Antigua all-inclusives below. Expect honest charm, attentive small-team service, and a slower pace — not a multi-restaurant resort with nightly entertainment. Closer in spirit to a high-end inn than a corporate AI.

One thing to know: the property is roughly 75 minutes from Maurice Bishop International (GND) at the south end of the island. The drive is part of the experience but is real driving — winding interior roads through villages and rainforest.

Destination 2

Anguilla

A different kind of trip entirely — Anguilla operates outside the all-inclusive model. Worth knowing about, but only worth pursuing if non-AI luxury is on the table.

About Anguilla as a destination

luxury beachfront, but not all-inclusive

Unlike Grenada and Antigua, Anguilla doesn't have an established all-inclusive resort category. The properties here — Cap Juluca, Four Seasons, Aurora, Malliouhana, Zemi Beach — operate as premium European Plan resorts, where rooms are billed separately from food and beverage.

What that means for this trip: a five-night stay for two rooms typically runs $1,000–$1,800 per room per night base on the secondary properties and $2,000+ on the marquee names. Food and drink is on top of that. It's a meaningfully different price point than the all-inclusive options on Grenada or Antigua.

Round-trip air for four: Est. $4,655 (American main cabin, IAH–AXA via MIA, roughly 8 hours each way).

also worth knowing

U.S. Virgin Islands

St. Thomas and St. Croix are on the table as well, with one real practical advantage worth flagging — no passport needed for U.S. citizens. The trade is that the all-inclusive scene here isn't their strong suit.

St. Thomas & St. Croix

passport-free Caribbean — different resort model

The USVI run on a different model than the AI-driven islands above. Most properties operate European Plan (room-only) or include a light continental breakfast. A handful of all-inclusive resorts exist, but they don't compete on scale or quality with what you'd get at the Antigua AIs.

What they do have is U.S. territory status — no passport required for U.S. citizens, just a current driver's license or birth certificate. That's a meaningful simplification if anyone in the party is short on a current passport or would rather not deal with an international entry process.

Round-trip air for the party of 4 from Houston:

  • St. Thomas (STT) — Est. $4,600, United, nonstop from IAH
  • St. Croix (STX) — Est. $3,062, one stop (the lowest air on the table)
