Your Voyage

From the Italian coast to the Aegean

Eleven nights aboard the Enchanted Princess, beginning in Rome and ending in Athens — with stops along the way in Sardinia, the gateway to Cinque Terre, the doorstep of Florence and Pisa, the medieval streets of Valletta, the Sicilian coast at Messina, and three Greek and Turkish ports that include Mykonos, Rhodes, and the ancient ruins of Ephesus.

This quote shows every available stateroom category — from the entry-level balconies on Decks 9 through 14, all the way up to the Penthouse Suite. Prices below reflect the full cabin cost for two guests sharing one stateroom.

Your itinerary

Civitavecchia (Rome)

Italy · Embarkation
Depart 6:00 PM
Board the Enchanted Princess at Civitavecchia, Rome's deep-water cruise port about an hour and a half northwest of the city. Settle into your stateroom, take a turn around the ship, and watch the Italian coast slide away as the sun sets over the Tyrrhenian Sea.

2
Wed · Oct 21

Sardinia (Alghero)

Italy
8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
Alghero feels more Catalan than Italian — the result of Spanish rule that left its mark on the language, the architecture, and the daily rhythm. Whitewashed buildings ring a small harbor, the old town is walled and walkable, and the coastline is famous for its pink coral.
Tender port — small boats shuttle guests ashore
Day
3
Thu · Oct 22

Genoa (Cinque Terre)

Italy
8:00 AM – 8:00 PM
Genoa is Italy's largest historic port and the gateway to the five villages of Cinque Terre — Monterosso, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola, and Riomaggiore — perched in pastel terraces above the Ligurian Sea. The villages are connected by a regional train; most visitors choose two or three rather than rushing through all five.
Day
4
Fri · Oct 23

Livorno (Florence & Pisa)

Italy
7:00 AM – 7:00 PM
Livorno is the working port that opens onto Tuscany. Florence is about 90 minutes inland by motorcoach or train — long enough to see the Duomo, walk the Ponte Vecchio, and stand in front of Michelangelo's David. Pisa is closer at 30 minutes, and the Leaning Tower is everything the photos suggest.
Day
5
Sat · Oct 24

Day at sea

Tyrrhenian Sea
A welcome breath between the busy Italian ports and the second half of the voyage. The pools, the spa, the lounges, the lecture series, and the long lunches on your private balcony are all yours. Pack a book; the Mediterranean obliges.
Day
6
Sun · Oct 25

Valletta

Malta
8:00 AM – 9:00 PM
Valletta is small, fortified, and entirely walkable from the cruise pier. The whole city is a UNESCO site — a 16th-century walled capital built by the Knights of St. John, with limestone bastions, baroque churches, and one of the most spectacular natural harbors in the Mediterranean. The late-evening departure means you can stay for dinner ashore.
Day
7
Mon · Oct 26

Sicily (Messina)

Italy
8:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Messina sits at the narrow strait separating Sicily from the Italian mainland. Two excursion options usually frame the day: Mount Etna, Europe's most active volcano, an hour and a half south; or Taormina, a hilltop town of Greek and Roman ruins with sweeping views of the coast and Etna in the distance.
Day
8
Tue · Oct 27

Day at sea

Ionian Sea
The crossing from Sicily into the Aegean. Watch the geography shift: Italian shoreline behind, Greek islands ahead. A perfect day for the spa, an unhurried lunch, or simply a chair on the open deck.
Day
9
Wed · Oct 28

Mykonos

Greece
7:00 AM – 9:00 PM
Whitewashed houses, blue-domed churches, and the famous windmills above the old town. The neighborhood called Little Venice is best in late afternoon, when the sun lowers over the water and the cafés along the seawall fill up. The long port day means there's no need to rush.
Day
10
Thu · Oct 29

Rhodes

Greece
9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
The Old Town of Rhodes is one of Europe's oldest continuously inhabited medieval cities — cobbled streets, the Palace of the Grand Master, and the Knights' Quarter, all inside intact 14th-century walls. About an hour south, the village of Lindos sits on a hilltop above a turquoise bay, crowned by an ancient acropolis.
Day
11
Fri · Oct 30

Kuşadası (Ephesus)

Türkiye
8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
Kuşadası is the seaside gateway to Ephesus, one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the Mediterranean. The ruins are about 30 minutes inland — the Library of Celsus, the Great Theatre, marble streets that once carried Roman senators and apostles. Excursions almost always include time at a Turkish carpet workshop or local market.
Day
12
Sat · Oct 31

Athens (Piraeus)

Greece · Disembarkation
Arrive 6:00 AM
The cruise ends at Piraeus, the ancient port that has served Athens for nearly three thousand years. The Acropolis, the Parthenon, and the Plaka neighborhood all sit about 30 minutes from the port. If your travel home allows it, an extra night or two in Athens is well worth it — most travelers regret not adding the time.
