From Margaret "Peggy" Hipple's planning notes

  • Dubrovnik / Kotor — city wall walk — need private ✱ — Montenegro — Bay of Kotor

Christina's expert take

  • "Need private" is the right call for Dubrovnik — a private guide sets the pace and knows the quiet routes. One honest note about the famous city wall walk, though: the full circuit is over a mile of medieval stone with hundreds of steps, uneven underfoot, and almost no shade or places to exit partway. It's the most demanding walk on this whole itinerary.
  • The good news: you don't need the walls to get the view they're famous for. The Mt. Srđ cable car delivers the postcard panorama — the walls, the old town, the sea — in four minutes, with no stairs at all. My suggestion: private guide at street level through the Stradun and old town (flat, polished, lovely), then the cable car for the view. If anyone wants a taste of the walls themselves, a short section near the Pile Gate entrance can be done and then exited — no need to commit to the full loop.

From Margaret "Peggy" Hipple's planning notes

  • Naples — Capri, Pompeii ✱ (private) ✱

Christina's expert take — can you do both in one day?

  • Honestly? Both in one port day isn't the trip you deserve. Pompeii is a 30–40 minute drive from the ship; Capri is a 50-minute boat each way plus island logistics. Squeezing both into one cruise day means rushing two of Italy's great places and betting your ship's departure on ferry schedules. Pick one anchor and do it well.
  • Pompeii (private) — the right anchor for this group. With a private guide, we choose a shorter, gentler route through the highlights — the streets are ancient uneven stone, so the guide's path-picking really matters — then pair it with a relaxed drive to Sorrento for lunch over the water. Reliable, unforgettable, and weather-proof.
  • Capri in November — here's the insider truth: it's the island's off-season. Boats can be cancelled by swell, the Blue Grotto is often closed, and many shops and restaurants shutter for winter. Capri deserves its own summer trip — save it, don't squeeze it.
  • A clever alternative worth knowing: Herculaneum — Pompeii's smaller sister city, buried by the same eruption. It's better preserved, far more compact, and needs half the walking. If Pompeii's size feels daunting, this is the elegant swap.
