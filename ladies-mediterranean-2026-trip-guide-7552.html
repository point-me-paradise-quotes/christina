🌿 How this trip is paced — please read first

This is a wonderfully full itinerary, and the secret to loving every day of it is simple: one anchor activity per day, then permission to rest. Every suggestion below follows that rule. Nothing on this trip requires you to rush, climb, or power through — the ship, a shaded café, or a bench with a view is always a perfectly good Plan B.

  • Mornings are your friend. Tour early while it's cool and quiet, be back aboard for a late lunch and a slow afternoon.
  • Sit-down lunches are part of the sightseeing. A long taverna lunch is the culture — never feel guilty about it.
  • Shoes matter more than anything you pack. Old-town Europe is marble, cobblestone, and polished stone. Broken-in walking shoes with real grip, every single day.
  • It's okay to split up. If two ladies want the city walls and two want a café on the harbor, that's a perfect day for everyone.

Arrive Athens — Welcome to Greece

Easy Day

Land in Athens at 4:35 PM and check into the Electra Metropolis, right in the heart of the city a short stroll from Plaka and Syntagma Square.

Tonight's Plan
  • Keep it simple: dinner at the hotel's rooftop restaurant with the Acropolis glowing above the city — no better welcome to Greece, and zero effort required.
  • Early to bed. Tomorrow is your one big Athens day, and jet lag is real on night one.
Friday · October 30

Athens — The Acropolis

Booked ✓
Confirmed Excursion
Complete Skip-the-Line Acropolis & Parthenon Tour with Guided Museum Visit

Your guide handles the tickets and the lines — you just soak it in. The path up the Acropolis is a steady uphill on marble that's been polished by 2,500 years of footsteps, so take it slow, use the handrails, and stop for photos often (the views are the best excuse to catch your breath).

For the Rest of the Day
  • Long, unhurried lunch in Plaka — the old quarter at the foot of the Acropolis. Pick a shaded courtyard taverna and order the grilled everything.
  • Afternoon rest at the hotel, then an easy evening stroll through Anafiotika, the tiny whitewashed island-village hidden inside Plaka's lanes.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: Do the tour in the morning while it's cool. If anyone prefers to skip the final climb to the top, the Acropolis Museum portion is world-class on its own.

Part Two · The Cruise
Sunday · November 1

Santorini — Oia on Your Own

Self-Guided Plan
YOUR PLAN · ASHORE ~9 AM · BACK ABOARD BY ~6 PM

The famous one — white villages balanced on the rim of a volcanic caldera, a thousand feet above the bluest water you've ever seen. Your plan is already the right one:

The Day, Step by Step
  • Breakfast on the ship, then tender ashore and take a taxi from the port up to Oia (~30–40 minutes of dramatic switchbacks — sit back and enjoy the show).
  • Wander Oia's marble lanes at your own pace — the blue-domed churches, the little galleries, the caldera views around every corner.
  • Lunch at Adami at Canaves Ena — a long, lovely, sit-down affair with the caldera spread out below you. This is the anchor of the day.
  • Taxi back down to the port by ~5:00 PM and tender back to the ship.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: Oia is gorgeous but uneven — marble steps and sloped lanes everywhere. Grippy shoes, take the lanes slowly, and agree on a café meeting spot in case the group naturally drifts apart. Skip anything labeled "steps to Ammoudi Bay" — that's 300 steep stairs you do not need.

Chania, Crete — Private Day

Tuesday · November 3

Katakolon — Ancient Olympia

To Book · Ship Excursion

Katakolon is a sweet, sleepy little port town whose whole purpose is Olympia — birthplace of the Olympic Games, about 40 minutes inland.

Recommendation
  • Book the Princess ship excursion to Ancient Olympia (per our plan). It handles the transfer, timing, and admission — perfect for this stop, where getting there is the whole logistics puzzle.
  • Once there, the good news: Olympia is one of the flattest, most stroll-friendly ancient sites in Greece — shaded paths through pine trees, ruins at ground level, benches along the way. Standing on the original Olympic stadium starting line is a genuine goosebump moment.
  • Prefer a no-tour day? Katakolon itself is a pleasant hour: one harborfront street of cafés and shops, perfect for coffee with a sea view and a slow morning aboard.
Wednesday · November 4

Corfu — Green Island Elegance

To Plan

Corfu is Greece with an Italian accent — Venetian arcades, French-style esplanades, and an old town that feels more like a small European capital than an island port. Note: this is a shorter port day, so plan one thing and do it well.

Suggestions (choose one)
  • Best fit: a private half-day driving tour (~4 hours) — Achilleion Palace, the empress Sisi's dreamy hilltop villa; the Kanoni viewpoint over Mouse Island; then drop-off in Old Town for lunch under the arcades of the Liston promenade. Minimal walking, maximum Corfu.
  • Simplest: taxi to Old Town on your own. The historic center is flat and compact — wander the arcades, peek into St. Spyridon church, sit for a long lunch, taxi back.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: Achilleion has some stairs and terraces — lovely, but let anyone who prefers wait in the gorgeous gardens. The views are democratic; they're everywhere.
Thursday · November 5

Split, Croatia — Diocletian's Palace

To Book · Private Tour

Split is unlike anywhere else: a living city built inside a Roman emperor's retirement palace. People shop, sip coffee, and hang laundry between 1,700-year-old columns. A later arrival today means a civilized morning aboard first.

Recommendation — We'll Arrange
  • Private 2-hour walking tour of Diocletian's Palace & Old Town with a licensed local guide, starting right at the port (it's a short, flat walk from the ship to the palace walls). The palace quarter itself is compact and level — this is one of the easiest great old towns in Europe.
  • After the tour: lunch on the Riva, Split's palm-lined seafront promenade, watching the harbor life go by.
  • If energy allows, the Green Market just outside the palace's Silver Gate is a wonderful browse — figs, lavender, local cheeses.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: The palace's stone streets are polished slick — this is the day for your grippiest soles. Skip the bell tower climb; the view from a Riva café table is the one you'll remember anyway.
Friday · November 6

Dubrovnik — The Pearl of the Adriatic

To Book · Private Tour

Dubrovnik's walled old town is one of the most beautiful urban spaces on earth — a marble-paved city sealed inside medieval walls, glowing honey-gold against the sea.

Recommendation — We'll Arrange
  • Private guide for the Old Town (~1.5–2 hrs): the Stradun main street, Rector's Palace, Franciscan Monastery with Europe's oldest working pharmacy. The Stradun itself is beautifully flat.
  • About the famous city wall walk — an honest word: it's spectacular, but it's roughly 1.2 miles with lots of steep stairs and no shade or exits partway. If the group wants it, do just the first stretch from the Pile Gate entrance and come back down — you'll get the postcard views without the full circuit.
  • The gentle alternative with even better views: the cable car up Mt. Srđ — four minutes up, a terrace café at the top, and the entire walled city and islands spread below you. All of the wow, none of the stairs.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: This is the trip's biggest "choose your adventure" day. Walls for the energetic, cable car for the wise, and a gelato on the Stradun for absolutely everyone.
Saturday · November 7

Kotor, Montenegro — Fjord of the South

To Plan

Today's show starts before breakfast: the ship glides deep into the Bay of Kotor, mountains rising straight from the water on both sides. Coffee on your balcony during the sail-in is not optional — it's the best free excursion of the entire cruise.

Suggestions (choose one)
  • Best fit: private boat to Our Lady of the Rocks & Perast (~half day) — a tiny man-made island with a blue-domed church in the middle of the bay, and the impossibly pretty baroque village of Perast. Almost all sitting, endlessly scenic, and very "us."
  • Simplest: Kotor Old Town on foot. It's right at the pier, flat, and pocket-sized — squares, cats (Kotor adores its cats), a small maritime museum, café tables under stone facades.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: You will see people climbing to the fortress above town — 1,350+ rough steps. Wave at them kindly from a café. That is not our climb.
Sunday · November 8

At Sea — Rest Day

Sea Day

Perfectly placed. Seven ports down, and the grand Italian finale ahead — today the only itinerary is recover and enjoy the ship.

Sea Day Menu
  • Sleep in. Long breakfast. Spa morning if you booked it (and if you didn't, today's the day to try).
  • Afternoon tea, a good book on the balcony, maybe the production show tonight.
  • One small task: lay out tomorrow's Naples plan and comfortable shoes tonight — it's an early arrival and the fullest day of the cruise.
Monday · November 9

Naples — Pompeii (& the Capri Question)

To Book · Private Tour

The big one. Naples is the gateway to both Pompeii and Capri — and here's my honest, love-you-like-family advice: doing both in one port day is a marathon (boats, funiculars, crowds, 10+ hours on your feet). Let's do one of them beautifully instead.

Recommendation — We'll Arrange
  • My pick for this group: Private Pompeii + Sorrento day. A private driver and licensed guide, a 2-hour highlights tour of Pompeii (not the exhausting full circuit — and we'll request the flatter, smoother "Pompeii for All" route, which was built exactly for comfortable walking), then a scenic drive to Sorrento for a long lunch above the sea and a little shopping before returning to the ship. Unforgettable, and paced like a vacation.
  • The alternative: a Capri day — hydrofoil across the bay, the Piazzetta, gardens of Augustus, lunch with a view. Magical, but be aware it involves boat transfers, the funicular, and more standing/queueing. Choose this only if the sea day left everyone feeling fresh.
🌿
Gentle-pace tip: Pompeii's original Roman streets are lumpy and rutted — this is the single most important day of the trip for sturdy shoes. A collapsible cane is genuinely smart here (and nobody at Pompeii will bat an eye; the Romans would have approved).

The Fine Print, Made Friendly

Small things that make a big trip easy

👟 Feet first

  • Two pairs of broken-in walking shoes with grippy soles — alternate days.
  • A collapsible cane packs flat and earns its keep at Pompeii and on marble lanes.
  • Compression socks for both long flights.

🧣 Late-fall Mediterranean

  • Think layers: light sweater + scarf + packable rain jacket. Days are mild, evenings cool.
  • Shoulders and knees covered for church visits (Vatican, St. Spyridon) — a big scarf solves everything.

💶 Money & phones

  • Euro everywhere except Croatia... which also uses the euro now. Easy!
  • Cards work nearly everywhere; carry ~€50 small bills for taxis, markets, and tips.
  • Turn on your carrier's international plan before departure.

🛳 Ship rhythm

  • Back-aboard time is sacred — plan to return an hour before all-aboard, every port.
  • Medallion on the watch strap = hands free all cruise.
  • Hydrate like it's your job. Port days are sneaky-long.
