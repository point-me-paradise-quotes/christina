The Ship

Symphony of the Seas

Oasis Class — a ship built like a small city, refreshed in 2023.

About the ship — highlights & quick facts

Symphony carries more to do than a week allows, which is exactly the point of two sea days on the way out. The ship is organized into distinct neighborhoods — an open-air Central Park planted with thousands of living trees and shrubs at its center, a Boardwalk with a carousel and the open-air AquaTheater at the stern, and a Royal Promenade running through the middle deck. You pick your pace: full-throttle on the sports deck or a quiet chair in the park with coffee.

Ultimate Abyss

The tallest slide at sea — a ten-story drop from the sports deck to the Boardwalk. You'll hear the screams before you see it.

Twin FlowRiders

Two surf simulators pushing 30,000 gallons of rushing water. Lessons available; spectating from the rail is a sport of its own.

Perfect Storm Waterslides

Three racing slides off the pool deck — warm-weather sailing in December means they run all week.

Glow-in-the-Dark Laser Tag

A full arena, and one of the additions that came with this ship's class. Book a session early on sea days.

AquaTheater & Ice Shows

High-dive and aerial performances at the stern, plus full productions on the ice rink — reserve show times in the app once aboard.

Playmakers Sports Bar & Arcade

Wall-to-wall screens and arcade games — the spot for bowl-season football while you're at sea Dec 21–22 and 26.

Sugar Beach

The candy and ice cream shop on the Boardwalk. Consider yourself warned.

Mini Golf & More

A full mini golf course with open ocean on every side, rock climbing walls, a zip line ten decks up, four pools, and 20 places to eat.

Why one guest costs more than half a double
Cruise lines price every stateroom on two guests. A solo traveler pays the full two-person fare, less only the second guest's taxes and port fees — so a room for one runs close to, or above, a room for two. It's industry-wide, not a quirk of this quote. If that math changes your thinking, see the note on a single room for all three, below the pricing.

Worth Considering

One stateroom for all three?

Because solo travelers pay nearly a full double fare, two rooms is the more expensive way to sail three people. Third guests share a stateroom at sharply reduced rates, so one balcony room for all three would usually cost meaningfully less than the two-room total above.

What you'd trade: the second bathroom, the private room, and the breathing space — which, on a seven-night Christmas sailing, is worth weighing honestly. If you'd like the exact one-room price to compare side by side, choose that option in the form below and I'll run it.

MSC Seascape · December 20–27, 2026

Same week, same three ports — a different ship, and a noticeably different number.

Both ships sail the identical Christmas week on the identical route: out of Galveston Sunday, December 20, Costa Maya on the 23rd, Christmas Eve in Roatan, Christmas Day in Cozumel, home Sunday the 27th. So this choice isn't about dates or ports — it's about the ship and the price. Symphony is the bigger, louder, more-to-do ship: the slides, the laser tag, the parks. Seascape, launched in 2022, is newer and sleeker, with a quieter, more European feel. Port hours differ by an hour or two — Symphony stays later in Cozumel on Christmas Day; Seascape stays later in Costa Maya. And the rates open lower here: the two staterooms together start at Est. $3,780.57 for all three — roughly $1,386 under the Symphony pairing, before gratuities on either ship.

Bella vs. Fantastica vs. Aurea — what MSC's tiers mean

Bella

The value fare — lowest price, room assigned by MSC
  • Every core inclusion: main dining room, buffet, shows, pools, kids & teens clubs
  • Dinner-seating preference can be requested
  • Discounted drink package available at booking
  • MSC assigns your exact cabin — possibly just days before sailing; view may be partial or obstructed
  • Seating requests filled after Fantastica and Aurea guests choose
  • Changing your cruise carries a fee

Fantastica

The pick-your-room fare — choice and location
  • You choose your exact stateroom and deck — which is why BR1 through BR4 step up the ship
  • First choice of dinner seatings, ahead of Bella guests
  • More flexible booking-change terms
  • Same ship, dining, and entertainment as Bella — you're paying for location and choice

Aurea

The wellness fare — MSC's top tier below suites
  • The best stateroom locations on board — the BGA's 172 ft² balcony is nearly the size of the room itself
  • My Choice dining: eat when you want, no fixed seating
  • Complimentary access to the Aurea Spa thermal area — saunas, steam rooms, relaxation lounges
  • Priority boarding in Galveston
  • Drinks are not included with Aurea — that's what the drinks + Wi-Fi toggle above adds, at the same flat $628 as every other category
